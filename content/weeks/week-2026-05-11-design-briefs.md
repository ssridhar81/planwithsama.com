## BRIEF 2 — WEDNESDAY REEL
**Tool:** Seedance (background video) + CapCut (text assembly)
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes
**Complexity:** Medium

### Seedance Prompt

> **Note on duration and approach:** Seedance generates clips of approximately 5–8 seconds. This week's scene (kitchen, morning, slightly hurried) suits a different approach from last week's static dusk room. Generate **two separate short clips** — one wide kitchen scene, one close-up detail — then cut between them in CapCut at the natural emotional midpoint. Alternatively, generate one clip and slow it to ~30% speed in CapCut for a dreamlike, time-stretched feel that matches the "how did it get to Wednesday" tone.

**Clip A — Wide kitchen scene (5–8 seconds)**
```
Generate a short ambient background video clip, approximately 5–8 seconds. 9:16 vertical format (1080x1920px).

Scene: A kitchen in early morning — approximately 7:45am. Soft grey-white light coming through a window, the kind that is bright but not quite day yet. The room is slightly undone: a school bag open on a chair and half-packed, a coffee cup abandoned mid-drink on the kitchen counter (still steaming very slightly), a torn-off sticky note with handwriting pinned under a fruit bowl, a phone face-down on the table. No people. No animals. The scene has a sense of being caught mid-flow — like someone just stepped out for a moment and will be back in 30 seconds.

Movement: Very slow, gentle pan across the kitchen counter from left to right — unhurried, almost imperceptibly slow. Starts on the coffee cup and drifts toward the window light. The movement suggests time passing without urgency.

Mood: Quietly hurried. Real. A Wednesday morning in an ordinary household that is managing more than it looks like from the outside.

Colour palette: Soft grey-white morning light from the window. Warm countertop surface tones. Cream, off-white, a few muted kitchen colours. Not bright. Not cold. The coffee steam adds one warm element.

Lighting: Diffuse morning window light as the primary source. Soft. Even. A little grey-blue from the sky outside.

Do not include: people, faces, hands, text, logos, pets, or screen glow from phone/devices.

Duration: 5–8 seconds.
Style reference: Quiet domestic realism. Shot like the opening frames of a short film — observational, non-intrusive.
```

**Clip B — Close-up detail (5–8 seconds, optional)**
```
Generate a short ambient background video clip, approximately 5–8 seconds. 9:16 vertical format (1080x1920px).

Scene: Tight close-up on the kitchen table surface. A phone face-down, screen just perceptibly lit up (light leaking from the edge, as if a notification arrived). Beside it, a sticky note with handwriting — too close to read, just legible enough to register as a list. Very shallow depth of field. The table surface and objects slightly warm-toned, the background kitchen out of focus.

Movement: Fully static. No camera movement. The only movement is the very faint pulse of the phone screen glow.

Mood: The weight of everything waiting. A Tuesday feeling that became a Wednesday.

Lighting: Soft diffuse morning light from off-screen window. No overhead fluorescent. Warm counter surface undertones.

Do not include: people, faces, readable text, logos.

Duration: 5–8 seconds, suitable for looping.
```

**CapCut assembly instruction for two clips:**
- Import Clip A and Clip B into CapCut timeline
- Use Clip A for 0:00–0:15 (first section of text — Sunday to Wednesday)
- Cut to Clip B at 0:15, use for 0:15–0:28 (the heavier second section — behind, not disorganised, holding too much)
- Loop Clip B if needed to reach 0:28
- **Alternative single-clip approach:** If only generating one clip, import Clip A, set playback speed to **30%** in CapCut (tap clip → Speed → 0.3x). A 7-second clip at 30% = ~23 seconds. Loop once for the remaining time.

---

### CapCut Text Overlay Spec

All text: centred, over the Seedance video.
Default font: DM Sans Light. Playfair Display italic for the emotional midpoint lines (noted below).
Default colour: White #FFFFFF with soft black text shadow (opacity 40%, blur 8px).
Text animation: Fade in 0.3s, fade out 0.2s between lines.
Position: centred horizontally, approximately 40% from top of frame.

| Timing | Text | Font | Size | Notes |
|--------|------|------|------|-------|
| 0:00–0:02 | Sunday: the plan was perfect. | DM Sans Light | 34px | Calm opening — give it a beat |
| 0:02–0:04 | Monday: mostly fine. | DM Sans Light | 34px | Same rhythm — building |
| 0:04–0:06 | Tuesday: one thing ran over. | DM Sans Light | 34px | Slightly ominous — same calm delivery |
| 0:06–0:08 | Wednesday: | DM Sans Light | 34px | Single word + colon — pause before the line lands |
| 0:08–0:11 | How did it get to Wednesday already? | Playfair Display italic | 40px | Tone shift — bigger, serif, emotional — this is the pivot |
| 0:11–0:13 | The dentist appointment isn't booked. | DM Sans Light | 30px | List begins — smaller, quieter, back to reality |
| 0:13–0:15 | The school form isn't sent. | DM Sans Light | 30px | Same rhythm |
| 0:15–0:17 | The thing from last week is still there. | DM Sans Light | 30px | Cut to Clip B here if using 2-clip approach |
| 0:17–0:19.5 | And she's already behind. | Playfair Display italic | 36px | Back to italic — emotional weight |
| 0:19.5–0:22 | Not because she's disorganised. | DM Sans Light | 32px | Reframe begins |
| 0:22–0:25 | Because she's holding too much. | Playfair Display italic | 38px | Emotional peak — biggest text of the reel |
| 0:25–0:29 | A week that runs away from you isn't a planning failure. It's a systems gap. | DM Sans Light | 28px | Split to 2 lines — closing statement, slower fade |
| 0:29–0:31 | [Endcard] | — | — | Fade to endcard overlay |

### Endcard spec
- Duration: 2 seconds (0:29–0:31)
- Background: Fade the video to Clay pale overlay (#F5EBE0 at 80% opacity) over final video frame
- Centre top: "Sama" in Playfair Display, Clay #C4855A, 48px
- Centre: planwithsama.com in DM Sans Light, Ink #2C2520, 28px
- Centre bottom: @planwithsama in DM Sans Light, Muted #8C8178, 24px
- No border, no pill — clean minimal

### Music direction
- Mood: Slightly more textured than last week's reel. Forward motion, but not stressful — like time moving quietly rather than running out.
- Tempo: 70–80 BPM. A little more presence than last week's near-silence. Something that pulses gently without pushing.
- Reference artists: Jon Hopkins (quieter end — "Abandon Window", "Open Eye Signal" opening bars), Nils Frahm ("Says"), Four Tet (ambient end)
- Instrumentation: Minimal piano with a very soft electronic pulse or textured pad underneath. Still warm, not clinical.
- Volume: 25–30% of max — same as last week. The silence between "Wednesday:" and "How did it get to Wednesday already?" must land completely clean.
- Where to find: CapCut royalty-free → "Ambient Electronic" or "Cinematic Pulse." Epidemic Sound → "Downtempo / Electronic" filtered to 70–80 BPM.

## BRIEF 4 — SUNDAY GRAPHIC
**Tool:** Claude Design

```

- No Sama wordmark on this graphic (Sunday graphic exception)
- Background: Dusk pale #F0EDF5 — full bleed, flat colour, no texture or gradient
- Primary text: Clay #C4855A (question)
- Secondary text: Muted #8C8178 (eyebrow, supporting line, handle)
- Typography: Playfair Display italic for the question, DM Sans light for everything else

LAYOUT — CENTRED, MINIMAL
Three elements only, all centred horizontally:

TOP (~25% from top):
Eyebrow in DM Sans light, Muted #8C8178, very small, letter-spaced, all caps:
"SUNDAY REFLECTION · @planwithsama"

CENTRE (vertically centred):
Main question in Playfair Display italic, Clay #C4855A, large:
"If someone could see everything you managed this week"
"— all of it —"
"what would they say?"
(Three lines, centred. Give the em-dash line its own line for breath. The question should feel generous and safe, not accusatory.)

BOTTOM (~75% from top):
Supporting line in DM Sans light, Muted, small:
"Leave your answer below. We read every one."

Handle at very base: "@planwithsama" in DM Sans light, Muted, small

VISUAL NOTES
- No wordmark, no slide number, no dot navigation, no divider, no CTA pill
- One subtle corner circle accent: Clay #C4855A at 6% opacity, bottom right only
- The question has three lines — give it more space than usual. It is the most open, generous question of the week.
- Dusk pale #F0EDF5 only — not Warm #F9F4EE, not Clay pale #F5EBE0
- If anything feels crowded, remove it. Four short lines. That is the whole graphic.
```

### Visual notes
- This week's question is the most emotionally open of the series so far — "all of it" is doing a lot of work. The three-line layout (with em-dash line isolated) gives it the pause it deserves.
- The "— all of it —" line set alone on its own line is a deliberate choice. Don't collapse it onto the line above or below.
- Keep the background completely flat. Dusk pale #F0EDF5 — no gradient, no texture.
