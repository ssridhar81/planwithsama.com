## LINKEDIN

### Post
I want to talk about a pattern I keep seeing in the data behind burnout in high-performing women.

It's not about what happens at work.

Most of the senior women I've worked with are genuinely thriving professionally. They have systems. They have clear frameworks. They know how to manage their workload.

The fracture point is always outside work.

Because the moment they close their laptop, they step into a second operation — with no tools, no systems and no support. Home. Family. Health. Personal admin. The invisible infrastructure of a life. And it is a full management job in itself.

The irony is sharp: these are often the same women who have helped their organisations adopt AI. Who understand what intelligent tooling can do. Who are living the productivity transformation professionally.

And then come home and plan the week in their heads.

The mental load is not a work problem. But it is a productivity problem. A leadership sustainability problem. A retention problem, if we're honest about it.

Women don't leave companies because of the job. They leave because the job plus everything else is no longer survivable.

Sama is being built to address the everything else.

Not a wellness app. Not a reminder tool. An AI co-pilot for the whole life — one that can hold the operational complexity of career, home, health, family and self simultaneously, and help surface what matters most on any given day.

We're in early build. But the problem is clear.

Are you thinking about whole-life cognitive load in your people strategy — or just the hours between 9 and 5?
